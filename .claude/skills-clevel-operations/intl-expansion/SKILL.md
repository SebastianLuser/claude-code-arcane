# International Expansion

Frameworks for expanding into new markets: selection, entry, localization, and execution.

## Market Selection Framework

### Scoring Matrix
| Factor | Weight | How to Assess |
|--------|--------|---------------|
| Market size (addressable) | 25% | TAM in target segment, willingness to pay |
| Competitive intensity | 20% | Incumbent strength, market gaps |
| Regulatory complexity | 20% | Barriers to entry, compliance cost |
| Cultural distance | 15% | Language, business practices, buying behavior |
| Existing traction | 10% | Inbound demand, existing customers |
| Operational complexity | 10% | Time zones, infrastructure, payments |

### Entry Modes
| Mode | Investment | Control | Best For |
|------|-----------|---------|----------|
| **Export** (sell remotely) | Low | Low | Testing demand |
| **Partnership** (reseller) | Medium | Medium | Markets with strong local requirements |
| **Local team** | High | High | Strategic markets with proven demand |
| **Entity** (subsidiary) | Very high | Full | Major markets, regulatory requirement |
| **Acquisition** | Highest | Full | Fast entry with existing base |

**Default path:** Export → Partnership → Local team → Entity.

## Localization Checklist

**Product:** Language, currency, payment methods, date/number formats, data residency, cultural adaptation.

**Go-to-Market:** Messaging adaptation, channel strategy, local case studies, local SEO.

**Operations:** Legal entity, tax compliance, employment law, customer support hours.

## Key Questions

- "Is there pull from the market, or are we pushing?"
- "Can we serve this market from HQ, or do we need boots on the ground?"
- "What's the regulatory timeline?"

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Prevention |
|---------|------------|
| Entering too many markets at once | Max 1-2 new markets per year |
| Copy-paste GTM from home market | Research local buying behavior |
| Underestimating regulatory cost | Assessment BEFORE committing |
| Hiring too early | Prove demand before hiring local team |

# /em:board-prep — Board Meeting Preparation

**Command:** `/em:board-prep <agenda>`

Prepare for the adversarial version of your board, not the friendly one. Every hard question they'll ask. Every number you need cold.

## The Preparation Framework

### Phase 1: Numbers Cold

**The numbers you must know without looking:**
- Current MRR / ARR and month-over-month growth rate
- Burn rate (monthly) and runway (months at current burn)
- Headcount by department
- CAC and LTV by channel / segment
- Net Revenue Retention
- Pipeline: value, conversion rate, average sales cycle
- Churn: rate, top reasons, top churned accounts

### Phase 2: Anticipate the Hard Questions

For every item on the agenda, generate the adversarial version of the question.

**Standard adversarial questions by topic:**

*Revenue:* "You missed revenue by 20% this quarter. What specifically failed?" / "Is this a pipeline, conversion, or capacity problem?"

*Runway:* "At current burn you have N months. What's your plan if the next round takes 9 months?" / "What would you cut first?"

*Product:* "You shipped X. What did customers actually do with it?" / "Where are you behind on roadmap?"

*Team:* "Who's at risk of leaving?" / "Is the team the right team for this stage?"

### Phase 3: Build the Narrative

1. **Where we are (honest)** — Current state, the real number
2. **What we learned** — What the data tells us that we didn't know 90 days ago
3. **What we got wrong** — Name it directly. Don't make them ask.
4. **What we're doing about it** — Specific, dated, owned actions
5. **What we need from this room** — Concrete ask, not "support"

**Bad news rule:** Never let the board be surprised. Send a 5-sentence email 3 days before.

### Phase 4: Adversarial Preparation

Do a mock board meeting. The questions that made you defensive = the questions you need to prepare for.

### Phase 5: Director-by-Director Prep

**For each director, know:** Their primary concern, the metric they watch, what would make them lose confidence, what they said last meeting.

## Pre-Meeting Checklist

**48 hours before:**
- [ ] All numbers verified against source systems
- [ ] Deck reviewed for internal consistency
- [ ] Pre-read sent to board
- [ ] One-on-ones done with any director likely to have concerns
- [ ] 3 hardest expected questions — rehearsed out loud

## During the Meeting

Name the hard thing before they do. "I want to address the revenue miss directly."

## After the Meeting

Within 24 hours: send action items with owners and dates. Note questions you weren't ready for. Schedule follow-up with any unsatisfied director.
